Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the world’s most accomplished and passionate cricketers. Known for his aggressive playing style, intense focus, and exceptional batting skill, Kohli is widely regarded as one of the best batsmen of his generation.

Early Life and Rise

- Kohli showed a passion for cricket from a young age and joined a cricket academy at nine. - He first came into the limelight in 2008, leading the Indian U-19 team to victory in the ICC U-19 Cricket World Cup.

International Career

- He made his ODI debut for India in 2008 and his Test debut in 2011. - Kohli quickly rose to prominence with his consistent and aggressive batting, especially in run-chases. - Known for his fierce competitiveness, he became a mainstay of the Indian lineup across all formats.

Captaincy

- Kohli took over as the Test captain in 2014 and later became the captain of the ODI and T20 teams in 2017. - He led India to numerous memorable wins, including overseas victories and a historic series win in Australia in 2021. - Under his leadership, India became a dominant team in all formats, though ICC titles eluded him.

Playing Style and Achievements

- Kohli is renowned for his precise technique, powerful strokes, and his ability to chase targets under pressure. - He has broken numerous records, including being the fastest to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 ODI runs. - Kohli was ranked as the world’s No. 1 batsman in both ODIs and Tests for several years.

IPL Success

- Kohli has played for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) since the IPL's inception in 2008 and became its captain in 2013. - Despite not winning an IPL title, he remains one of the tournament's highest run-scorers.

Personal Life

- Kohli is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, and the couple has a daughter named Vamika. - Off the field, he’s known for his fitness, style, and philanthropy. Kohli's passion, work ethic, and relentless drive to succeed have made him a global cricket icon. He continues to inspire fans with his intensity, commitment, and love for the game.
